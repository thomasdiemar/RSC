Bounded Preemptive Simplex (Minimization) with Bounds — Objective & Net Evaluation Row

Geometry produces torque-cancel locks: t1 = t2, t3 = t4.
We minimize z = -(t1 + t2 + t3 + t4) with bounds 0 ≤ t_i ≤ 1.
Keep bounds inside simplex (LB/UB/B statuses). Nonbasics sit exactly at LB or UB.

Step 0 — Initial Tableau (minimization)

Locks as rows; choose t1 and t3 basic, t2 and t4 nonbasic at LB (0).
Objective: z = -2·t2 - 2·t4 ⇒ reduced costs: r(t2) = -2, r(t4) = -2. (For minimization, LB can enter if r < 0.)

Key points about the objective & reduced costs (minimization)

  • Eligibility: A nonbasic at LB may enter if r < 0; a nonbasic at UB may enter if r > 0.
  • After a pivot: the entering variable’s reduced cost becomes 0; the z-RHS changes by (old r_enter) × θ.
  • Bound hits (no pivot): if the opposite bound of the entering wins the ratio test strictly, you just update values and z-RHS; the tableau coefficients stay the same.

Final throttles: t1 = t2 = 1, t3 = t4 = 1 (all within [0,1]); torque is cancelled by locks; minimized objective z* = -4 (equivalently, total thrust = 4).
