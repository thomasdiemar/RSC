\section*{Bounded Preemptive Simplex with Priority Goals (Full Tableaux)}

Geometry (updated):
\[
r_1=(0,2,0),\quad a_1=(-100,0,0),\qquad
r_2=(0,-4,0),\quad a_2=(-100,0,0),
\qquad 0\le t_1,t_2\le 1.
\]

Force and torque maps:
\[
A=\begin{bmatrix}
-100 & -100\\ 0 & 0\\ 0 & 0
\end{bmatrix},
\qquad
B=\begin{bmatrix}
0&0\\ 0&0\\ 200&-400
\end{bmatrix}.
\]

Thus
\[
F=\begin{bmatrix}-100(t_1+t_2)\\0\\0\end{bmatrix},\qquad
T=\begin{bmatrix}0\\0\\200\,t_1-400\,t_2\end{bmatrix}.
\]

% ================================================================
\section*{Stage $P_1$: Minimize Torque (L1)}

Introduce deviational variables $\tau^+, \tau^-\ge 0$:
\[
200t_1 - 400t_2 + \tau^- - \tau^+ = 0,
\qquad
\min z_1 = \tau^+ + \tau^- .
\]

\subsection*{P1 Tableau 0 (start)}
Make $\tau^+$ basic:

\[
\begin{array}{c|r|rrrr|c}
\text{Basic} & \text{RHS} & t_1 & t_2 & \tau^- & \tau^+ & \text{Status}\\
\hline
\tau^+ & 0 & +200 & -400 & +1 & 1 & \B\\
\hline
z_1 & 0 & \mathbf{+200} & \mathbf{-400} & \mathbf{+2} & 0 &
\end{array}
\]

Nonbasic variables at start:
\[
t_1:\ \LB,\qquad
t_2:\ \LB,\qquad
\tau^-:\ \LB.
\]

Minimization rule:
- A nonbasic at \LB{} can enter if $r_j<0$.  
Here $r_{t_2}=-400<0$ $\Rightarrow$ $t_2$ enters.

\subsection*{P1 Tableau 1 (degenerate pivot: $t_2$ enters, $\tau^+$ leaves)}

Solve the goal row for $t_2$:
\[
t_2=\tfrac{1}{2}t_1+\tfrac{1}{400}\tau^- - \tfrac{1}{400}\tau^+.
\]

\[
\begin{array}{c|r|rrrr|c}
\text{Basic} & \text{RHS} & t_1 & t_2 & \tau^- & \tau^+ & \text{Status}\\
\hline
t_2 & 0 & \tfrac{1}{2} & 1 & \tfrac{1}{400} & -\tfrac{1}{400} & \B\\
\hline
z_1 & 0 & \mathbf{0} & 0 & \mathbf{+1} & \mathbf{+1} &
\end{array}
\]

This shows:
\[
z_1 = 0\quad\text{whenever}\quad \tau^\pm=0,\ \text{and}\ 200t_1-400t_2=0,
\]
i.e.
\[
\boxed{t_1 = 2t_2}.
\]
So torque can be driven to zero: $Z_1^*=0$.  
Lock this as a hard relation for $P_2$.

% ================================================================
\section*{Stage $P_2$: Force objective with $P_1$ locked}

With $t_1=2t_2$ and $0\le t_1,t_2\le1$, the effective bound is
\[
0 \le t_2 \le 0.5,\qquad t_1=2t_2.
\]

% ---------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection*{Variant A: Maximize $+x$ force}

\[
z_2 = F_x = -100(t_1+t_2) = -300\,t_2,\qquad \max z_2.
\]

\subsubsection*{P2-A Tableau}
\[
\begin{array}{c|c|c|c}
\text{Var} & \text{Value} & \text{Status} & \text{Reduced cost}\\
\hline
t_2 & 0.0 & \LB\ ([0,0.5]) & -300\\
t_1 & 0.0 & \B\ (t_1=2t_2) & \text{--}
\end{array}
\]

Maximization rule:
- Nonbasic at \LB{} can improve only if $r_j>0$.  
Here $r_{t_2}=-300<0$ $\Rightarrow$ increasing $t_2$ makes $z_2$ worse.

\[
\boxed{t_1=t_2=0,\quad F=(0,0,0),\ T=(0,0,0).}
\]

% ---------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection*{Variant B: Maximize thrust in $-x$ direction}

Instead maximize $-F_x=+300t_2$.

\subsubsection*{P2-B Tableau A (start)}
\[
\begin{array}{c|c|c|c}
\text{Var} & \text{Value} & \text{Status} & \text{Reduced cost}\\
\hline
t_2 & 0.0 & \LB\ ([0,0.5]) & +300\\
t_1 & 0.0 & \B\ (t_1=2t_2) & \text{--}
\end{array}
\]

Bounded ratio test (preemptive):
\[
t_1=2t_2\le 1 \Rightarrow t_2\le 0.5.
\]
So opposite-bound distance is $0.5-0=0.5$.  
No pivot needed: \emph{preemptive UB hit}.

\subsubsection*{P2-B Tableau B (after UB hit)}
\[
\begin{array}{c|c|c}
\text{Var} & \text{Value} & \text{Status}\\
\hline
t_2 & 0.5 & \UB\ ([0,0.5])\\
t_1 & 1.0 & \B\ (t_1=2t_2)
\end{array}
\]

Optimality (max):
- Nonbasic at \UB{} could only help if $r_j<0$.  
Here $r_{t_2}=+300>0$ $\Rightarrow$ decreasing $t_2$ reduces $z_2$.

\[
\boxed{t_1=1,\quad t_2=0.5,\quad F=(-150,0,0),\ T=(0,0,0).}
\]
