\section*{Problem}

Decision variables with bounds:
\[
0 \le x_1 \le 4, \qquad 0 \le x_2 \le 3.
\]

Hard constraint:
\[
2x_1 + 3x_2 + s_c = 12 \quad (s_c \ge 0).
\]

Goal 1 (priority $P_1$): meet $x_1 + x_2 = 5$ with minimal \emph{underachievement}
\[
x_1 + x_2 + d_1^- - d_1^+ = 5, \quad \text{minimize } d_1^-.
\]

Goal 2 (priority $P_2$): keep $2x_1+x_2 \le 8$ with minimal \emph{overachievement}
\[
2x_1+x_2 + d_2^- - d_2^+ = 8, \quad \text{minimize } d_2^+.
\]

We begin at the bound-feasible point $x_1=x_2=0$ so that
\[
s_c = 12,\quad d_1^- = 5,\quad d_2^- = 8.
\]

Columns are ordered as
\[
[x_1,\; x_2,\; s_c,\; d_1^-,\; d_1^+,\; d_2^-,\; d_2^+].
\]

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\section*{Tableau 1: Initial}

\[
\begin{array}{c|r|rrrrrrr}
\text{Basic} & \text{RHS} & x_1 & x_2 & s_c & d_1^- & d_1^+ & d_2^- & d_2^+ \\
\hline
s_c     & 12 & 2 & 3 & 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
d_1^-   &  5 & 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 & -1 & 0 & 0 \\
d_2^-   &  8 & 2 & 1 & 0 & 0 &  0 & 1 & -1 \\
\hline
P_1=z_1 &  0 & -1 & -1 & 0 & 0 & \phantom{-}1 & 0 & 0
\end{array}
\]

Here, at the start, $r_{x_2}=-1<0$ so \emph{increasing} $x_2$ (from its lower bound) reduces $d_1^-$.

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\section*{Tableau 2: After preemptive slide $x_2:0\rightarrow 3$ (no pivot)}

Increase $x_2$ until a limit is reached. Bounds allow $x_2 \le 3$, so we slide directly to $3$:
\[
x_2=3,\qquad s_c=3,\qquad d_1^-=2,\qquad d_2^-=5.
\]

\[
\begin{array}{c|r|rrrrrrr}
\text{Basic} & \text{RHS} & x_1 & x_2 & s_c & d_1^- & d_1^+ & d_2^- & d_2^+ \\
\hline
s_c     &  3 & 2 & 3 & 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
d_1^-   &  2 & 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 & -1 & 0 & 0 \\
d_2^-   &  5 & 2 & 1 & 0 & 0 &  0 & 1 & -1 \\
\hline
P_1=z_1 & -2 & -1 & -1 & 0 & 0 & \phantom{-}1 & 0 & 0
\end{array}
\]

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\section*{Tableau 3: After Pivot 1 (enter $x_1$, leave $s_c$)}

A pivot on the $(s_c,x_1)$ entry gives:

\[
\begin{array}{c|r|rrrrrrr}
\text{Basic} & \text{RHS} & x_1 & x_2 & s_c & d_1^- & d_1^+ & d_2^- & d_2^+ \\
\hline
x_1     & 1.5 & 1 & 1.5 & 0.5 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
d_1^-   & 0.5 & 0 & -0.5 & -0.5 & 1 & -1 & 0 & 0 \\
d_2^-   & 2   & 0 & -2   & -1   & 0 &  0 & 1 & -1 \\
\hline
P_1=z_1 & -0.5 & 0 & 0.5 & 0.5 & 0 & \phantom{-}1 & 0 & 0
\end{array}
\]

We have $x_1=1.5$, $x_2=3$, $d_1^-=0.5$. To push $d_1^- \downarrow 0$, we must \emph{decrease} $x_2$ off its upper bound (its reduced cost $+0.5$ for $P_1$ means that decreasing it will lower $z_1$).

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\section*{Intermediate Tableau: Bound-Exchange Pivot (enter $x_2$ by decreasing)}

We now pivot on the $(d_1^-,x_2)$ entry (value $-0.5$) to bring $x_2$ into the basis and send $d_1^-$ out:

\[
\begin{array}{c|r|rrrrrrr}
\text{Basic} & \text{RHS} & x_1 & x_2 & s_c & d_1^- & d_1^+ & d_2^- & d_2^+ \\ 
\hline
x_1   & 3 & 1 & 0 & 0 & -1 & \phantom{-}1 & \phantom{-}1 & -1 \\
x_2   & 2 & 0 & 1 & 0 & \phantom{-}2 & -2 & -1 & \phantom{-}1 \\
d_2^- & 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 & -2 & \phantom{-}2 & \phantom{-}1 & -1 \\
\hline
P_1=z_1 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & (\text{locked}) & & &
\end{array}
\]

This tableau represents the point $(x_1,x_2)=(3,2)$ with
\[
d_1^-=0,\quad d_1^+=0,\quad d_2^-=0,\quad d_2^+=0,\quad s_c=0.
\]
Thus the goal of priority $P_1$ (minimizing $d_1^-$) has reached the best possible value of~$0$.

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\section*{Final $P_1$-Optimal Tableau (one clean basis)}

A convenient $P_1$-optimal basis is $\{x_1,x_2,s_c\}$:

\[
\begin{array}{c|r|rrrrrrr}
\text{Basic} & \text{RHS} & x_1 & x_2 & s_c & d_1^- & d_1^+ & d_2^- & d_2^+ \\
\hline
x_1 & 3 & 1 & 0 & 0 & -1 & \phantom{-}1 & \phantom{-}1 & -1 \\
x_2 & 2 & 0 & 1 & 0 & \phantom{-}2 & -2 & -1 & \phantom{-}1 \\
s_c & 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 & -4 & \phantom{-}4 & \phantom{-}1 & -1 \\
\hline
P_1=z_1 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & (\text{locked}) & & &
\end{array}
\]

Since $d_1^-=0$ is fixed (optimal for $P_1$), we check $P_2$:
\[
2x_1+x_2 = 2\cdot 3 + 2 = 8 \quad\Rightarrow\quad d_2^+=0,
\]
which is optimal subject to keeping $d_1^-=0$.

\section*{Conclusion}

The lexicographically optimal solution is
\[
x_1 = 3,\qquad x_2 = 2,\qquad s_c=0,
\]
with all deviational variables zero. Thus both goals are satisfied at their best possible levels.
