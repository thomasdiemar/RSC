We consider a spacecraft with six thrusters $T_1,\dots,T_6$, each with
a scalar thrust level
\[
  u_i \in [0,1], \quad i=1,\dots,6.
\]
The position vectors $r_i$ and thrust directions $a_i$ are
\begin{align*}
  T_1 &: r_1 = (-1,-1,-1), & a_1 &= (0,0,1),\\
  T_2 &: r_2 = ( 1,-1,-1), & a_2 &= (0,0,1),\\
  T_3 &: r_3 = ( 0, 1,-1), & a_3 &= (0,0,1),\\[0.5em]
  T_4 &: r_4 = (-1,-1, 1), & a_4 &= (0,0,-1),\\
  T_5 &: r_5 = ( 1,-1, 1), & a_5 &= (0,0,-1),\\
  T_6 &: r_6 = ( 0, 1, 1), & a_6 &= (0,0,-1).
\end{align*}

\section*{Net Force (Priority 1: Eliminate Force)}

The net force is
\[
  \bm{F} = \sum_{i=1}^6 u_i a_i
         = (0,0,F_z), \quad
  F_z = u_1+u_2+u_3 - u_4 - u_5 - u_6.
\]
Priority 1 requires zero net force,
\[
  F_z = 0
  \quad\Longrightarrow\quad
  u_1+u_2+u_3 = u_4+u_5+u_6.
\tag{F}
\label{eq:force}
\]

\section*{Torque (Priority 2: Maximize $t_x$)}

The torque from thruster $i$ is
\[
  \bm{\tau}_i = r_i \times (u_i a_i),
\]
and the total torque
$\bm{\tau} = (t_x,t_y,t_z)$ is obtained by summing the contributions.
A straightforward cross product computation yields
\begin{align*}
  t_x &= -u_1 - u_2 + u_3 + u_4 + u_5 - u_6,\\
  t_y &= \phantom{-}u_1 - u_2 - u_4 + u_5,\\
  t_z &= 0.
\end{align*}
Under the constraints \eqref{eq:force} and $0 \le u_i \le 1$,
the maximum achievable value of $t_x$ is
\[
  t_x^{\max} = 2.
\]
Thus, for Priority 2 we impose the additional goal
\[
  t_x = 2.
\tag{T$_x$}
\label{eq:tx}
\]

\section*{Minimizing $t_y, t_z$ (Priority 3)}

The torque component $t_z$ is identically zero for any feasible
$\bm{u}$. Hence, Priority 3 reduces to minimizing $|t_y|$
subject to \eqref{eq:force}, \eqref{eq:tx}, and $0 \le u_i \le 1$.

Solving this system, one convenient family of solutions with
$t_y = 0$ is
\[
  u_6 = 0,\quad u_3 = 1,\quad
  u_4, u_5 \in \left[\tfrac12,1\right],\quad
  u_1 = u_4 - \tfrac12,\quad
  u_2 = u_5 - \tfrac12.
\]
Any choice in this family satisfies
\[
  \bm{F} = \bm{0}, \qquad
  t_x = 2, \qquad
  t_y = 0, \qquad
  t_z = 0.
\]

A particularly simple solution (with minimal total thrust) is
\[
  (u_1,u_2,u_3,u_4,u_5,u_6)
  = (0,0,1,0.5,0.5,0).
\]
We verify:
\begin{align*}
  \text{Force: } & u_1+u_2+u_3 = 0+0+1 = 1,\\
                 & u_4+u_5+u_6 = 0.5+0.5+0 = 1
                 \;\Rightarrow\; \bm{F} = (0,0,0),\\[0.5em]
  \text{Torque: } &
  t_x = -0 -0 +1 +0.5 +0.5 -0 = 2,\\
  & t_y = 0 -0 -0.5 +0.5 = 0,\\
  & t_z = 0.
\end{align*}

Thus, the thruster settings
\[
  T_1 = 0,\quad
  T_2 = 0,\quad
  T_3 = 1,\quad
  T_4 = 0.5,\quad
  T_5 = 0.5,\quad
  T_6 = 0
\]
satisfy the bounded preemptive goal structure:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Zero net force,
  \item Maximal $t_x$,
  \item Minimal $t_y$ and $t_z$ (both equal to zero).
\end{enumerate}
