Objective & Net-Evaluation Row — How it’s computed (Minimization)

  • Reduced costs: r_j = c_j − c_B^T B^{-1} a_j. For an LB nonbasic, eligibility is r_j < 0.
  • Net objective change at a step: if entering variable has reduced cost r_{enter} before the step and ART picks step θ, then Δz = r_{enter} \cdot θ.
  • After a row pivot: the entering variable becomes basic ⇒ its reduced cost becomes 0 (its column is now part of the basis).
  • Coupled equalities (C₁,C₂): when a nonbasic enters, its paired basic moves with the same θ, effectively doubling the contribution ⇒ e.g. r(t₂)=−2 and r(t₄)=−2 in P2.
  • ART with bounds/equalities: compare row limits (basics hitting LB/UB) and opposite bound of the entering var; also include “locked” equalities such as Fx=50 (which restricts Σt). The smallest permissible θ wins.
